# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students and researchers gathering credible material for assignments and study. They search across approved academic sources, inspect native previews, save useful results, organise them into workspaces, and ask Alexander for help using their collected context.

## Product Purpose

StudyLib makes academic research easier to trace and organise. Success means a user can search trusted sources, understand where each result came from, preview it without losing their place, save it to the right workspace, and continue their research without duplicate results or unclear loading states.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, atmospheric, and dependable. StudyLib should feel like a candlelit archive built for concentrated work: warm and distinctive, but always readable and practical.

## Anti-references

- Generic Bootstrap blue controls or an interchangeable modern dashboard aesthetic.
- Decorative textures or illustrations so faint that users must strain to see them.
- Heavy visual effects that compete with reading, searching, or source inspection.
- Search flows that leave the product for a vendor page, hide source provenance, or appear to load forever.

## Design Principles

1. Keep research provenance visible: every result identifies its source and opens through the appropriate native or protected viewer.
2. Make progress legible: searches, previews, failures, and exhausted results always reach a clear state.
3. Preserve the archive atmosphere without sacrificing contrast, responsiveness, or interaction clarity.
4. Use progressive disclosure: show the strongest result from each selected source first, then reveal deeper ranks on request.
5. Keep the user's workspace and research context stable across navigation and reloads.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast for text and controls. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus, semantic labels, reduced motion, smaller laptop screens, and clear status text that does not rely on color alone. Decorative artwork remains hidden from assistive technology and cannot block interaction.
